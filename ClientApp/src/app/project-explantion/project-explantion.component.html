This project contains the source code for team atofeedback for the govhack 2018 competition.

Govhack page: https://2018.hackerspace.govhack.org/team_management/teams/27

Github entry: https://github.com/munisravan/atofeedback



We are team atofeedback and for our govhack entry we have developed a customer service tool to help the ATO surface information and provide this to the customer.

This year are trying to tackle the The Friendly ATO challenge and the Bounty: Tax Help Centers.


As the information is entered the system starts to build a profile of the user and use this to surface questions and answers that similar people have asked before .

This will create a predictive list of insights based on previous interactions with the ATO.

  • Based on the job, age, postcode and other information we can fit you in to a profile.

  • Previous people that fit your profile asked these questions next

  • Offer suggested answers.



Positive effects for the community:

  • More efficient use of support worker time, increasing customer throughput and satisfaction.

  • More interactive, personalized answers to questions will result in higher customer satisfaction due to a more organic conversation.

  • Reduction in return calls due to ‘insights’ surfacing multiple questions in the same call.

  • The ability to predict what questions the customer may have allows the support worker to prepare in advance, providing more accurate answers and reducing the amount of time a customer is put on hold.

  • Data collected from this service can be analyzed to determine what information needs to be created or updated, as well as highlight areas where additional training or marketing might be useful.


Information supplied by user and related data:

Occupation

  • Occupation type

Location/Postcode (Specified or via location services)

  • Average salary

  • Socioeconomic advantage/disadvantage

  • Index of education and occupation

  • Index of economic resources

Age

  • Age range/group

  • There are probably more identifier factors that can be derived from the supplied information, but from a quick look at the data and an eyeball at what data would be relevant, it’s been limited to these few.

  • A more thorough investigation by an experienced data analytics expert would more accurately identify important data.

  • ATO doesn’t currently have any tags/metadata for information, e.g. ‘Deductions’, or ‘Low-income’. If they did have this information it would be possible to group questions/answers based on the metadata.


Justification:

  • There are similar existing applications of AI, supplying targeted information based on profiling a user, used in advertising. EBay and Amazon have targeted advertising and recommended items. Google advertising has targeted advertisements. If it’s possible to use this technology to supply users with targeted advertisements, we can also leverage it to supply users with targeted useful information.

  • Just as targeted advertisement can lead users to products they weren’t aware they wanted/needed at that point in time, we can do the same with useful information, reducing the amount of return calls required and increasing the overall usefulness of each call.
